When people think of bees, the majority will think of a honeybee. A large colony of bees ruled by one queen, living in a hive, and producing honey.

Or, some might think of a bumble bee; but a bumble bee as a singular species, not many.

Most will be surprised to learn that Virginia has somewhere around 450-500 bee species. Why not a specific number? We will get to that later)

Being keyed for your state doesn't mean that bee is in your state, but that it has a good chance of being there. So it may have been there in the past (historical record) or it is found in a surrounding state and has the potential to be there.

I went through the genera keyed for Virginia and pulled all possible species out!
